# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters, hiring managers, and data science peers evaluating Asif Sayyed's skills, projects, and professional experience. They land on the page with a specific question: can this person deliver? They scan quickly, looking for evidence of depth, not decoration.

## Product Purpose

A personal brand portfolio that converts visitors into opportunities. Every section exists to answer one question: what has Asif built, what does he know, and why should someone work with him. Success is a contact form submission, a LinkedIn connection, or an interview invite.

## Brand Personality

Professional, approachable, to the point. The voice is confident without arrogance, technical without jargon. Rooted in the ethos of a problem solver who finds opportunity in chaos (Sun Tzu). The tone is direct, warm, and earned no filler, no fluff.

## Anti-references

- Glassmorphism, blurred backdrops used decoratively
- Generic SaaS templates (hero-metric, identical card grids, gradient text)
- AI-generated visual cliches (overgradiented, overcarded, overanimated)
- Overdesigned portfolios that prioritize flash over substance

## Design Principles

- **Show, don't just tell.** Working projects, live data, real credentials speak louder than claims. The portfolio is a collection of evidence, not adjectives.
- **Data-driven storytelling.** Every section serves a narrative arc. The page flows from identity to work to credentials, each piece reinforcing the others.
- **Expert confidence.** Clean, uncluttered, purposeful. Nothing exists without a reason. White space is strategic, not empty.
- **Professional approachability.** Technical depth without intimidation. A hiring manager should feel informed; a peer should feel impressed.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA target. Dark theme with sufficient contrast ratios. Respects reduced motion preferences. Semantic HTML and keyboard-navigable structure.
